# Common Ground — Voice Guide

The most distinctive thing about Common Ground is its voice. For our audience —
parents of children on the spectrum — voice *is* the product. They've been talked
at by clinicians, teachers, insurance reps, and well-meaning strangers for years.
What makes them stay on this site instead of bouncing is the feeling that someone
on the other side actually understands.

Don't let that get edited out by an engineering pass.

## Canonical voice

The canonical reference is `/support/caregiver`. When uncertain, copy that
page's rhythm:

> "You are not failing. You are carrying something most people will never
> understand."

> "Asking for support is not giving up. It is how you keep going."

## Capitalization

Use **sentence case** everywhere — capital first letter of every sentence and
heading, normal sentences underneath. Proper nouns stay capitalized inside
sentences.

| Use sentence case for                    | Examples                                |
| ----------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Page titles, section headings             | "Your plan, friend."                    |
| Card titles                                | "When today feels heavy"                |
| Button and link labels                     | "Build my plan", "Got it", "Learn more" |
| Body copy                                  | "We'll keep it simple. One moment."     |
| Eyebrow / uppercase tags                   | "Your Pathfinder", "Start here"         |

Do **not** use Title Case ("Build My Plan") or all-lowercase ("build my plan")
in our copy — sentence case is the rhythm of the canonical caregiver page.

### Proper nouns to always capitalize

Common Ground · Texas ABA Centers · Pathfinder, Pathfinders · Still Waters ·
Care Plan · Home Base · Parent Support · Hard Days · Harris Center · ARD ·
IEP · 504

## Rules

1. **No emojis as praise.** Navigational emojis on category cards are fine.
   Streak fire icons, "you crushed it!", "way to go!" — none of that.
2. **Write like a friend who has been here, not a coach.**
   - Not: "You crushed your check-in goal!"
   - Yes: "This is hard. You've been showing up anyway."
3. **Never call the parent the patient.** They are the person holding
   everything together.
4. **No clinical claims.** Common Ground does not diagnose, treat, or
   evaluate. It points. It mirrors. It introduces.
5. **CTAs are invitations, not commands.**
   - Not: "Submit", "Get started!", "Get my matches"
   - Yes: "Build my plan", "Start when you're ready", "Try a check-in", "Join the waitlist"
6. **Short paragraphs.** Two sentences max in most places. One is often enough.
7. **Both things can be true.** Some of the strongest lines acknowledge two
   feelings at once. Use that pattern.
8. **No streak shaming.** If a parent hasn't come back in a while, the right
   line is "You may need more support today" — never "You're behind."

## Specific copy preferences

| Avoid                                | Prefer                                       |
| ------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------- |
| "Caregiver wellness · Live"          | "Your check-in pattern this week"            |
| "Welcome back, friend"               | "Demo: Friday afternoon, 5-day pattern"      |
| "5-day check-in streak"              | "5-day check-in pattern"                     |
| "Get my parent matches"              | "Join the waitlist"                          |
| "Score" / "points" / "rating"        | "Support readiness", "support tank"          |
| "You're behind"                      | "You may need more support today"            |
| "You're in the Watch zone"           | "This week's been heavier. You've still been showing up. Both things are true." |
| "Submit"                             | "Build my plan", "Continue"                  |
