# Common Ground — Voice Guide

The most distinctive thing about Common Ground is its voice. For our audience —
parents of children on the spectrum — voice *is* the product. They&rsquo;ve been talked
at by clinicians, teachers, insurance reps, and well-meaning strangers for years.
What makes them stay on this site instead of bouncing is the feeling that someone
on the other side actually understands.

Don&rsquo;t let that get edited out by an engineering pass.

## Canonical voice

The canonical reference is `/support/caregiver`. When uncertain, copy that
page&rsquo;s rhythm:

> "You are not failing. You are carrying something most people will never
> understand."

> "Asking for support is not giving up. It is how you keep going."

## Rules

1. **Lowercase headings**, except where typography hierarchy genuinely needs
   sentence case. UI labels and chrome are lowercase.
2. **No emojis as praise.** Navigational emojis on category cards are fine.
   Streak fire icons, "you crushed it!", "way to go!" — none of that.
3. **Write like a friend who has been here, not a coach.**
   - Not: "You crushed your check-in goal!"
   - Yes: "this is hard. you&rsquo;ve been showing up anyway."
4. **Never call the parent the patient.** They are the person holding
   everything together.
5. **No clinical claims.** Common Ground does not diagnose, treat, or
   evaluate. It points. It mirrors. It introduces.
6. **CTAs are invitations, not commands.**
   - Not: "Submit", "Get started!", "Get my matches"
   - Yes: "build my plan", "start when you&rsquo;re ready", "try a check-in", "join the waitlist"
7. **Short paragraphs.** Two sentences max in most places. One is often enough.
8. **Both things can be true.** Some of the strongest lines acknowledge two
   feelings at once. Use that pattern.
9. **No streak shaming.** If a parent hasn&rsquo;t come back in a while, the right
   line is "you may need more support today" — never "you&rsquo;re behind."

## Specific copy preferences

| Avoid                                | Prefer                                       |
| ------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------- |
| "Caregiver wellness · Live"          | "your check-in pattern this week"            |
| "Welcome back, friend"               | "demo: friday afternoon, 5-day pattern"      |
| "5-day check-in streak"              | "5-day check-in pattern"                     |
| "Get my parent matches"              | "join the waitlist"                          |
| "Score" / "points" / "rating"        | "support readiness", "support tank"          |
| "You&rsquo;re behind"                       | "you may need more support today"            |
| "You&rsquo;re in the Watch zone"            | "this week&rsquo;s been heavier. you&rsquo;ve still been showing up. both things are true." |
| "Submit"                             | "build my plan", "continue"                  |

## When to break the rules

Headings that are proper nouns (Texas ABA Centers, Common Ground, Still Waters,
Pathfinders) keep their casing. Crisis messaging uses normal sentence case
because lowercase reads as low-stakes — and 988 is high-stakes.
